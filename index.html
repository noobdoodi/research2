Child Labor Research Essay

Child labor was, and still is a problem of the world. Countries like Eritrea still have children doing work without pay. It is also rated as the No. 1 worst country for child labor. 265 million, or 17%, of children have been subjected to child labor. An increase in child labor is on the rise and it begun way before COVID hit the world.
Child Labor in the USA
Child labor in the USA was most important because of the agricultural economy of the original 13 colonies. In the 1900s, 18% of US workers were under the age of 16. Children worked as indentured servants on family farms for other people, Trade was learn't via apprenticeship that started between the ages of 10-14. When the Irish Potato Famine struck in the 1840s, the Irish moved to the USA to fill low level factory jobs. According to History.com "In the 1880s, groups from southern and eastern Europe arrived, providing a new pool of child labor workers".
